Cantuccini o Biscotti di prato

Description

Very simple italian cookie. That is quick to make and more importantly,
does not transform the whole kitchen in to a "No mans land".

Ingredients

  • 250g flour
  • 2-3g baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • Zest of 1 lemon or orange
  • 120-175g sugar (depends on how sweet you want them
    or how sweet are your additions)
  • One of three options:
    1. 3 eggs
    2. 2 eggs + 55g butter
    3. 2 eggs + 40g ghee
  • One of these options:
    1. 120g almonds
    2. 40g poppy seeds + 100g raisins
    3. 120g raisins
    4. 100g rough chopped dark chocolate (at least 60% cacao)

Steps

  1. Take a deep bowl, place it on a scales and sift in it the flour,
    the baking powder and the salt.
  2. Now take another bowl, put it on scales (don't forget to press tare)
    and in it zest the citrus with microplane and add the sugar.
  3. Depending on the option chosen add 1 egg or the butter or ghee and
    start mixing using electric mixer. Start slow and go to high.
    After 30 seconds add the rest of the eggs.
  4. Turn on the oven to 180℃.
  5. Once the mixture is homogeneous and aerated (should take
    about 2-3 minutes of mixing) add the additives (nuts/dry fruits/chocolate)
    and using a silicone spatula mix them in.
  6. Sift in the flour in to the wet ingredient bowl and using
    silicone spatula mix them together.
  7. Transfer the dough on to baking sheet lined with baking paper
    and shape the dough in to a log (use wet hands to help you).
  8. Once the oven is hot enough, place and bake cookie log for 25-30 minutes
    (the log should have gained some color).
  9. Remove cookie log from the oven and turn down the oven to 160℃.
  10. After 10 minutes remove the cookie log on to a cutting board
    (be careful, it is still hot) and cut the log about 3cm apart.
  11. Place them on one side back on the baking sheet and put them back
    in to the oven for 10-20 minutes (depends on how crunchy you want the
    outside to be.
  12. After the time is up remove and flip them on to the other side and
    bake them again for 10-20 minutes (this time should be shorter).
  13. Remove and place them on a wire cooling rack (no soggy bottoms)
    and after another 15 minutes they should be cool enough to enjoy with
    coffee.
